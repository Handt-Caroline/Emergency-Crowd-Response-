HOW IT WORKS
STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

From SOS tap to
hospital arrival

Here's exactly how ECRS turns a bystander's panic into a coordinated, professional medical response — in under 30 seconds.

📱 Bystander
01
🆘
Press the SOS Button
The bystander opens ECRS and taps the large red SOS button. No login, no registration — the app is instantly ready. Designed for maximum speed under stress.
02
🏥
Select Emergency Type
Choose from Medical, Fire, or Security. Currently, Medical is active. Fire and Security modules are coming soon. One large tap — no scrolling required.
03
📋
Describe the Situation
Select the specific medical situation — from cardiac arrest and severe bleeding to childbirth or allergic reaction. 13 situation types available in English and French.
04
👥
Indicate Number of Victims
Tap 1, 2, 3+, or Unknown. This data helps the hospital prepare the right level of triage response before anyone arrives.
05
📷
Add Info & Photo (Optional)
Optionally provide a name, phone number, and scene photo. All fields can be skipped — the system still works with zero personal info. The photo gives the hospital a real-time view of the scene.
06
📍
Tap SEND ALERT
Pressing Send Alert triggers GPS capture. The app requests the device's current location with high accuracy and bundles it with all alert data.
GPS · navigator.geolocation API
⚡ ECRS System
07
🌐
Alert Posted to Backend
The alert is sent as a POST request to /api/alerts on the ECRS Node.js server with all collected data, including GPS coordinates and optional photo.
Node.js · Express · Multipart/JSON
08
🔎
Dispatch Engine Activates
The DispatchEngine calculates the distance from the bystander's GPS position to every registered, available hospital. It ranks them by proximity and capacity, then selects the optimal candidate.
DispatchEngine.js · Haversine Formula
09
📡
Alert Pushed to Hospital via WebSocket
Using Socket.IO, ECRS instantly pushes the full alert — type, situation, victims, distance, contact, photo URL, and suggested preparation — to the selected hospital's private room. This happens in real time, no polling needed.
Socket.IO · emergency:new event
10
⏳
Bystander App Shows Loading State
While the hospital responds, the bystander sees a loading screen. A socket listener on the alert room waits for alert:confirmed, alert:reassigned, or alert:failed.
Socket.IO · alert room listener
🏥 Hospital Dashboard
11
🚨
Dashboard Flashes — Alarm Sounds
The hospital's ECRS dashboard switches from standby to full alert mode. An alarm sound plays via the Web Audio API. The alert panel shows all emergency details: type, situation, victims, distance, contact info, photo, and suggested preparation.
Socket.IO · emergency:new · AudioContext
12
🗺️
Hospital Reviews & Sees the Map
A Leaflet.js map shows the exact bystander location with a pin. The hospital staff reviews the situation, reads preparation suggestions, and decides whether to accept or decline the alert.
Leaflet.js · GPS pin · Prep suggestions
13
✅
Hospital Confirms the Alert
The hospital taps CONFIRM. This triggers alert:confirmed back to the bystander's socket room with the hospital name, phone, distance, and GPS for directions.
confirmAlert() · socket.emit · alert:confirmed
14
🗺️
Bystander Gets Directions
The bystander's screen updates to show the confirmed hospital name, distance, a Google Maps directions link, and the hospital's phone number. The patient can now be transported with confidence.
15
🔄
Fallback: Reassign if Declined
If a hospital declines, the Dispatch Engine automatically contacts the next closest available hospital. The bystander accepts or all options are exhausted.
declineAlert() · DispatchEngine · alert:reassigned

Three Possible Outcomes
The system handles every scenario gracefully
✅
Confirmed

A hospital accepts. The bystander receives the hospital name, distance, phone number, and a Google Maps directions link. Case is logged.

🔄
Reassigned

A hospital declines. ECRS automatically tries the next closest available hospital, keeping the bystander informed throughout.

📞
No Hospital

If all hospitals are unavailable, the app shows direct emergency call numbers (SAMU 15, Pompiers 18, Police 17) as an immediate fallback.
